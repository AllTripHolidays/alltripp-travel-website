Title: Evergreen Temples of India – Timeless Beauty in Stone

India’s temples are not just places of worship; they are living stories carved in stone. Their architecture, history, and spiritual depth leave even the busiest minds stilled.

🔹 **Brihadeeswara Temple, Tamil Nadu**  
Known as the “Big Temple,” this Chola masterpiece stands not just in grandeur but in harmony with cosmic geometry. Built in the 11th century, it’s a testament to Dravidian architecture and ancient engineering marvels.

🔹 **Jagannath Temple, Puri**  
Famous for the Rath Yatra, this Odisha temple represents devotion in motion. The massive chariot festival is more than a ritual – it’s a spectacle of unity and belief.

🔹 **Kedarnath Temple, Uttarakhand**  
Tucked away in the Himalayas, reaching here feels like both a physical and spiritual journey. Dedicated to Lord Shiva, it stands defiant against weather and time.

🔹 **Meenakshi Temple, Madurai**  
A riot of colors, sculptures, and stories. This temple is a celebration of Goddess Meenakshi and showcases the artistic brilliance of the Nayak dynasty.

Temples like these are evergreen – not just because they endure, but because they continue to grow roots in every soul that visits.
